Samarkhand Uzbekistan

samarkhand is one of the oldest-cities in center Asia and is the second-largest city in Uzbekistan.oday, it is listed as ‘Samarkand ⁠— Crossroads of Cultures’ in the UNESCO World Heritage, and visitors marvel at its jaw-dropping architecture, vast history, and archeological treasures.Founded in the 7th century BC, Samarkand is the ancient beating heart and one of the main highlights of Uzbekistan. First heading

By the time of the Achaemenid Empire of Persia, it was the capital of the Sogdian satrapy. The city was conquered by Alexander the Great in 329 BCE, when it was known as Markanda, which was rendered in Greek as Μαράκανδα.[6] The city was ruled by a succession of Iranian and Turkic rulers until it was conquered by the Mongols under Genghis Khan in 1220. Today, Samarkand is the capital of Samarqand Region and a district-level city, that includes the urban-type settlements Kimyogarlar, Farxod and Xishrav.[7] With 551,700 inhabitants (2021),[8] it is the second-largest city of Uzbekistan.
